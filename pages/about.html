Fayzan Irfan,
F.C.C.M.

MD Candidate · Researcher · Founder · London, UK

A globally oriented medical professional working at the intersection of clinical medicine, research, and innovation.

Born in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia and raised in Dubai, I was educated under the UK curriculum at The Winchester School before pursuing medicine internationally. I am currently completing my MD at Kyrgyz State Medical Academy (KSMA) in Bishkek, Kyrgyzstan — integrating evidence-based medicine with global clinical standards.

Alongside my clinical training, I am pursuing the Fellowship in Critical Care Medicine (F.C.C.M.) — an internationally recognised, IAOTH UK-accredited programme deepening my expertise in ICU management, ventilator care, hemodynamic monitoring, and emergency medicine.

I am deeply committed to medical research and its translational impact on patient outcomes. My ongoing narrative review on Cardiac Amyloidosis reflects a growing focus on cardiovascular medicine, cardiac imaging, and modern therapeutics.

Beyond research, I have taken on significant institutional leadership — founding the Medical Research & Publication Society at KSMA and serving as Executive Head of the Student Council. I co-founded Aurea DD as Founding COO, a health innovation venture building at the intersection of medicine and entrepreneurship.

My professional interests span Critical Care Medicine, Emergency Medicine, Psychiatry, and Dermatology. Long-term goals include PLAB, MRCP (UK), and advanced postgraduate training in the United Kingdom, grounded in clinical excellence and ethical leadership.
