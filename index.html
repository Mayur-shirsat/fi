WHO I AM

I am a globally oriented medical professional currently pursuing my Fellowship in Critical Care Medicine (F.C.C.M.), an IAOTH UK-accredited program that builds advanced expertise in the management of critically ill and emergency patients. This training has strengthened my proficiency in advanced life support, ventilator management, hemodynamic monitoring, ABG interpretation, electrolyte and acid–base balance, sepsis management, and multi-organ failure. It continues to refine my clinical judgment in high-acuity ICU settings where precision and empathy are vital.

My academic foundation is shaped by a multicultural background, having been born in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, raised in Dubai, and educated following the UK curriculum at The Winchester School. I am currently pursuing my MD at Kyrgyz State Medical Academy (KSMA) in Bishkek, where I integrate evidence-based medicine with global clinical standards.

Alongside clinical training, I am deeply committed to medical research and its impact on improving patient outcomes. My ongoing narrative review, "Cardiac Amyloidosis in the Modern Era: Pathophysiology, Diagnosis, and Evolving Therapeutic Strategies", reflects my growing interest in cardiovascular medicine, cardiac imaging, and modern therapeutics.

My professional interests span Critical Care Medicine, Emergency Medicine, Psychiatry, and Dermatology, with long-term goals that include pursuing PLAB, MRCP (UK), and advanced postgraduate training grounded in clinical excellence and ethical leadership.
